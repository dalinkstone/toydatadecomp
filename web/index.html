Bayesian approach: Given a customer has purchased item Y with a digital coupon N times, we can use Bayesian updating to estimate P(purchase | reduced discount). If the posterior probability exceeds a threshold, we reduce the discount. The prior is the base purchase rate; the likelihood is the observed coupon-purchase frequency. This gives us a mathematically grounded way to optimize coupon values per customer-product pair.

Why Conservative Wins

The math: A 1-2% lift on $21.5B in front store revenue = $215-430M. That is real money. It does not need to be $2B to matter.

The stock market has already priced in the decline ($6.47 → $3.66 → $1.39 EPS). If we project conservative numbers and beat them:

  1. Stock re-rates upward on earnings surprise
  2. Management credibility increases
  3. Recommendation system gets more investment

If we project aggressive numbers and miss: credibility erosion, program gets cut.

Given CVS's declining EPS, margin compression, and store closures, any projection above "moderate" would not be credible to investors or the board.

What Happens If We Do Nothing

  • Coupon waste continues: 65% of clips go unredeemed
  • Hidden gems stay hidden: High-margin products remain undiscovered
  • Organic stars get discounted: Uniform promotions destroy margin on products that don't need help
  • No personalization: Every customer sees same offers
  • Competitive gap widens: Retailers with recommendation systems continue pulling ahead
